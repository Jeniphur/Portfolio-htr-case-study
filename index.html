Project Overview

People who are interested in fostering, volunteering, or adopting animals through Happy Tails Rescue, a non-profit animal rescue shelter, need a website that provides them with easily accessible information in an efficient, engaging, and stress-free manner. In doing so, users will be more likely to work with Happy Tails Rescue.

Design Process & Goals

Our team's goals:

  • Increase the level of accessibility on both web and mobile
  • Simplify text, information architecture and user flow
  • Highlight the organization’s goals
  • Create an experience that will allow users to trust Happy Tails Rescue

My Role

This project was created by myself and 3 partners. Although we equally worked on this project, my primary efforts shined during our ideation and wireframe sections.

Research

Objective

Our research objective was to gain empathy for users going through, or having already gone through, the process of adopting an animal. Additionally, we also needed to know Happy Tails Rescue’s business goals.

We began by creating a survey on Google Forms and we each distributed the survey via social media. The survey received 41 responses. Based on those responses, we picked 7 surveyors to interview.

Our interview goals:

  • Understand what information users look for when considering adopting a pet
  • Understand what the users end goals are when visiting an adoption/rescue website/li>
  • Understand how a user might decide which shelter they want to foster from or donate to

Interviews & Insights

From our 41 survey responses, 7 user interviews and 1 stakeholder interview, our team was able to pull and organize insights to better understand everyone’s stories.

Survey key takeaways:

  • 61% of people would be interested in fostering one day
  • The most important things on an adoption website are adoption information, agency details and animal help services
  • The top 3 factors people look for when considering adopting is animal personality, temperament (towards kids/other animals) and medical history

User interview key takeaways:

  • Wants easy to digest information along with ease of access
  • Meeting an animal is a priority factor in adopting
  • Needs the adoption process, fees and contact information to be clearly laid out

Stakeholder interview key takeaways:

  • Prioritize finding people to foster and adopt animals
  • Recently commissioned their logo and would prefer the logo to stay the same
  • Expressed a need for a more mobile friendly website

Ideation

Brainstorming

Before creating wireframes, our team brainstormed features that would help the Happy Tails website. After compiling a list of sticky notes, we selected ideas that would be the most valuable and realistic to the users and organization.

Heuristic Evaluation

While we were brainstorming, our team conducted a heuristic evaluation. We discovered the Happy Tails Rescue website did not pass accessibility tests; the text would be difficult to read to some users. We went through their website as a team and found that users who aren’t tech savvy may have difficulty navigating because of the lack of call-to-action buttons and visual hierarchy.

User Flow & information Architecture

Our team went through the Happy Tails Rescue website and reorganized their information architecture. We cleaned up any links that were repetitive and combined similar pages to save users a click. There were 4 links in their main navigation and 19 links in their side navigation. We were able to nestle links within the side navigation into the main navigation. This decision makes it easier for users to navigate and it lessens the total amount of links.

Wireframes

Website & Mobile Mockups

Based on all of our insights and ideation, our team made separate low-fidelity wireframes for the desktop homepage. Then, we did A/B testing to determine which navigation we felt would be the most effective for our users. Later, we followed the same process in making the mobile version of the website.

Testing

Objective

The goals of our user tests were to answer the following questions:

  • Is our site’s navigation intuitive and simple to use?
  • Is the user able to complete their tasks without distraction or confusion?
  • Does our user understand our pages and the information on them?

Key Takeaways

We conducted 5-7 user tests for both the web and mobile prototypes. Based on our user tests, we discovered there were several visual and technical issues we needed to iterate on.

Final

Current Prototype

With our current prototype, users feel invited to Happy Tails Rescue. They are able to create trust which encourages users to adopt, foster, volunteer, and donate. Our team feels it displays the organization’s goals with confidence.
